University Of Mumbai conducts Seminar in Nepal

Nepali students spend millions of dollars on higher education and travel to the United Kingdom, United Nations, and European countries for Education. In the past few years, Nepalese have realized that quality education is available in neighboring country India as well. According to stakeholders, India is the third-largest country in higher education.



The University of Mumbai is one of the oldest and wisest university in India. The University of Mumbai, also known as the Mumbai University, is a collegiate public state university in Mumbai, Maharashtra, India. With over 800 affiliated colleges, the University of Mumbai is one of the world's largest universities. Established in the year 1857, the University of Mumbai has always attracted students from all over the world who are looking for the best education and colleges.

The University of Mumbai (formerly known as the University of Bombay) is one of India's oldest and most prestigious universities. Suhash Pednakar, Vice-Chancellor of the University of Mumbai, stated that he has a policy in place to help his students become self-sufficient, responsible, and mature citizens. He also said at the event held at the Royal Singi Hotel in Kathmandu, "Our university is committed to creating and maintaining world-class facilities." Nepali students consider themselves to be citizens of their own country.


According to Jitesh Naulakha, Nepal Representative and Chief Ambassador of the University, the University of Mumbai currently has 12 special centers with 56 departments, 781 affiliated colleges, two main campuses, and a School of Engineering and Applied Sciences. According to him, the number of young Nepali students at Mumbai University has increased through years . According to Naulakha, the university's strengths are teaching-learning, research, consulting, and innovation. Pratik Gandhi, Chief Executive Officer, Edulab Educational Exchange Pvt. says that because of their proximity to language and culture, Nepali students are better off in Mumbai than in other countries for higher education. We are opening the door for Nepali students!



When came in conversation with Dr. Sunil S. Patil, Director, Department of Student Development

What is the main purpose of your visit to Nepal?
My main goal in Nepal is to educate students in Nepal about the University of Mumbai and reassure them of its strengths. We teach a variety of subjects in faculties such as Science and Technology, Commerce, and Humanities, which are the primary subjects for Nepali students studying in other countries. Through this visit, we hope to welcome Nepali students to our university.

What special subjects are available at Mumbai University?
Our university specializes in vocational subjects in particular. We have a distinct identity in biotechnology, science, and other fields, and our Department of Foreign Languages is another highlight of our university. Our identity is also inspired by music and theater academies.

Is there any facility available for Nepali students?
We charge lower fees to students from SAARC countries than to students from other countries. SAARC students must pay twice as much as Indian students, and students from other countries must pay five times as much. Because there is a one-stop-shop for scholarships, students should apply through the embassy.

The University of Mumbai endeavors to provide higher-quality education and welcomes students from Nepal and other nations to accomplish their ambitions.
